Which One Is Better: Animal Crossing New Horizones or Pocket Camp?

Decide For Yourself!

Gameplay Mechanics

New Horizons

Animal Crossing: New Horizons, available on Nintendo Switch, offers players a full-fledged and immersive gaming experience. Players are placed on a deserted island, where they have the freedom to customize and shape the island landscape, create unique designs, and interact with charming animal villagers. The game operates in real-time, mimicking the time and seasons of the real world, bringing a sense of immersion and progression to the player's island life. With various activities like fishing, bug catching, and fossil hunting, players can earn Bells and resources to expand their island and create their dream home.

Pocket Camp

On the other hand, Animal Crossing: Pocket Camp, available on mobile devices, offers a more streamlined and compact gameplay experience. Players take on the role of a campsite manager, where they can customize and decorate their campsite, as well as build relationships with animal visitors. The game follows a time-based cooldown system, where players can fulfill requests from visiting animals to earn rewards and craft furniture items. Pocket Camp focuses on bite-sized tasks and is designed for short play sessions on the go, making it ideal for players who prefer a casual and mobile gaming experience.

Scope and Customization

New Horizons

Animal Crossing: New Horizons, available on Nintendo Switch, offers players a full-fledged and immersive gaming experience. Players are placed on a deserted island, where they have the freedom to customize and shape the island landscape, create unique designs, and interact with charming animal villagers. The game operates in real-time, mimicking the time and seasons of the real world, bringing a sense of immersion and progression to the player's island life. With various activities like fishing, bug catching, and fossil hunting, players can earn Bells and resources to expand their island and create their dream home.

Pocket Camp

In contrast, Animal Crossing: Pocket Camp offers a more limited scope for customization. While players can decorate their campsite and camper van with furniture and accessories, the design options are more straightforward compared to New Horizons. Additionally, players can change their character's appearance and outfit, but the level of customization is not as extensive as in the console version.

Multiplayer and Social Interaction

New Horizons

Animal Crossing: New Horizons places a strong emphasis on multiplayer and social interaction. Players can visit each other's islands locally or online, trade items, and even invite friends to join in on fun activities like fishing and bug-catching. Multiplayer in New Horizons allows for collaborative island designs and strengthens the sense of community among players.

Pocket Camp

Animal Crossing: Pocket Camp also incorporates social features, such as visiting friends' campsites and exchanging kudos. However, the interactions are more limited compared to New Horizons. Pocket Camp focuses more on completing tasks and helping visiting animals, with less emphasis on direct multiplayer activities.

In summary, Animal Crossing: New Horizons offers a deep and immersive gaming experience on Nintendo Switch, with extensive customization, real-time gameplay, and robust multiplayer options. Animal Crossing: Pocket Camp, on the other hand, is a mobile adaptation that provides a more streamlined and casual experience, perfect for short play sessions on the go. Each game caters to different play styles, making them both enjoyable additions to the beloved Animal Crossing franchise.
