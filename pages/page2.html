Helpful Reminders and Tricks for Skills

Fishing

As an aspiring angler, fishing is an art that every villager must master. Armed with a fishing rod, players can cast their line into rivers, ponds, and the vast ocean to reel in a diverse array of fish. Catching different fish at various times of the day and throughout the year adds a thrilling sense of discovery. Not only is fishing a relaxing pastime, but it also rewards players with Bells and contributes to museum exhibits, creating a rich and diverse display of aquatic life.

Catching Bugs

With a trusty bug-catching net in hand, players can explore their island's flora and fauna to discover a delightful assortment of insects. Each bug has its own unique habitat and time of day it appears, adding an element of excitement and challenge. Capturing bugs helps diversify museum collections, brings in Bells for island improvement, and sparks a sense of appreciation for the natural beauty found in every nook and cranny of the island.

Gathering Fruits

The design and layout of a player's home are crucial in shaping the island's character. Players can explore their interior design skills by customizing furniture, rearranging rooms, and selecting decor to create a unique and inviting abode. The creativity extends to the great outdoors as well, where players can design and personalize their island landscape, crafting paths, gardens, and outdoor spaces that reflect their taste and style.

Designing Homes

Bells are the currency of the Animal Crossing world, and players must master various money-making methods to fund island development. Selling collected items like fish, bugs, and fruits at Nook's Cranny is a simple way to earn Bells. Additionally, players can participate in daily money rock-hitting and money tree planting for a windfall of riches. Successful island management ensures a steady flow of Bells, supporting the growth and improvement of the island community.
